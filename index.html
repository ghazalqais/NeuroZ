The tools to measure tremor have existed for years. The failure isn't technological — it's structural. Medication is titrated against a 5-minute clinical snapshot, then the patient goes home and manages a condition that shifts hourly with food, sleep, and stress.

NeuroZ Poise closes this loop. Every meal generates a calibrated tremor record. Every week builds a longitudinal curve. By the next appointment, the neurologist isn't guessing — they're reading 30 days of evidence.

The prescription improves. The patient eats. Both problems are solved by the same hardware.

The system

Robotics that stabilize.
AI that understands.

01
6-axis IMU captures tremor
The embedded inertial measurement unit samples motion at 200Hz across all 6 degrees of freedom — acceleration and rotation. Every tremor event is timestamped and classified by frequency and amplitude.
200Hz · 3-axis accel · 3-axis gyro
02
Microcontroller corrects in <8ms
The onboard processor calculates the inverse correction vector and drives the servo actuators to cancel tremor motion before it reaches the bowl. The physics loop runs at 125Hz — faster than the tremor frequency.
<8ms latency · real-time servo loop
03
AI builds the clinical record
Every session is uploaded to the NeuroZ platform. The AI classifies tremor severity by meal, builds a 30-day longitudinal curve, flags medication window correlations, and generates a prescription-ready report for the neurologist.
30-day history · medication correlation
